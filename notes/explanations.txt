A desktop app that loads the trained ANN model and predicts energy output from user inputs.

Demonstrates:
1. Your trained models can be exported from Colab.
2. The models can be loaded in a separate Python application.
3. The same scalers and feature order are used correctly.
4. Users can enter new data and get predictions.
5. The app supports two real datasets.
6. The app shows model performance metrics.
7. The app validates bad input values.

Application:
A user enters weather or building information.
The trained ANN processes the values.
The app outputs predicted solar energy or heating load.

PVGIS Solar Output Mode:
1. Solar planning
A homeowner, student, or small installer can estimate whether a location and time condition will produce low, moderate, or high solar output.

2. Energy scheduling
If predicted output is high around midday, users can schedule energy-heavy tasks during that time, such as charging batteries or running appliances.

3. Renewable energy education
Teachers/students can see how sun height, time of day, temperature, and wind speed affect solar generation.

4. Feasibility
Before installing solar panels, users can explore whether the expected energy output is reasonable for their location.

UCI Heating Load mode:
1. Building design comparison
Architects, engineers, or students can compare how different building shapes, surface areas, roof areas, wall areas, and glazing affect heating demand.

2. Energy-efficient design
If the predicted heating load is high, the design may need improvements, such as reducing heat loss, changing glazing, or modifying building geometry.

3. Early-stage simulation alternative
Instead of running complex energy simulation software, the ANN gives a quick estimate.

4. Academic demonstration
It shows how ANN can learn energy-related patterns from building features and support energy efficiency decisions.

It answers two practical questions:
Solar mode:
"Given these weather and time conditions, how much solar energy might be produced?"

Heating load mode:
"Given this building design, how much heating energy might the building require?"

The UI is not a replacement for professional engineering software or actual solar monitoring systems. It is a prototype that demonstrates how trained ANN models can support quick energy prediction and educational analysis.
